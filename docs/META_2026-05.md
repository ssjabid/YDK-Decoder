## How to read each entry
- **End board** = what they typically leave up going first (this is the Board-Breaker target).
- **Choke** = the single card to Ash / Imperm / Belle — stop this and they stumble.
- **First** = how to play when YOU go first.
- **Second** = how to break their board when you go second (and *how* to remove the boss).
- **Handtraps/side** = the cards that shine in the matchup.

## Branded / Despia — *tier 1 fusion midrange*
- **How it wins:** Albaz Fusion spells (Branded Fusion, Branded in Red/White) summon a wall of Fusion bosses, often on YOUR turn, then grind with recursion + burn.
- **End board:** Mirrorjade the Iceblade Dragon · Masquerade the Blazing Dragon · Branded in Red (→ Guardian Chimera on your turn) · Dramaturge of Despia.
- **Choke:** **Branded Fusion** — but they bait Ash with Branded Opening / Lubellion first, so save Ash for the *real* one. Aluber is the secondary target. **Droll** kills the search chain.
- **First:** Few hard negates → a clean board usually beats them. Don't over-commit LIGHT/DARK into open **Super Polymerization**; respect **Bystials** banishing your LIGHT/DARK.
- **Second:** Mirrorjade punishes removal — if it leaves the field *by your card* it wipes your monsters at the End Phase. Out it with **non-destruction**: your own **Super Polymerization** (eats it, dodges the wipe), banish/bounce, or spend its banish first. Clear Branded backrow.
- **Handtraps/side:** Ash (real Branded Fusion), Droll, Nibiru, Super Polymerization, Bystial Druiswurm.
- **Confidence: high** (won Spain Nationals May 23; Top 32 France).

## Dracotail (Branded) — *tier 1 fusion dragons*
- **How it wins:** Dracotail Faimena is a near-one-card starter (discard → Fusion Summon), chaining Dracotail fusions + a trap suite; often splashes Branded for Mirrorjade.
- **End board:** Dracotail Mululu (sets a trap + negates a face-up monster) · Dracotail Shaulas · Dracotail Arthalion · Mirrorjade · set Dracotail Flame/Horn/Sting.
- **Choke:** **Dracotail Faimena** / the first Fusion — deny it and they often have nothing. **Ghost Belle** is excellent (shuts their Trap activations too).
- **First:** Their disruption is bounce + negate, not destruction. Summon key monsters in **Defense** to dodge Dracotail Horn (bounces Attack-position monsters).
- **Second:** Mass monster removal (Raigeki/Dark Hole/DRNM) is **weak** — answers live in hand/GY/backrow. Stop backrow + deny Fusion fuel. Out Mirrorjade via Super Poly / banish.
- **Handtraps/side:** Ghost Belle, Ash, Retaliating "C", Dimension Shifter, Droll.
- **Confidence: high** (won Asia WCQ Thailand May 24; Top 4 Singapore).

## Mitsurugi — *tier 1 Reptile Ritual control*
- **How it wins:** One starter (Ame no Habakiri) → a wall of recurring Ritual monsters + searched traps, grinding behind repeated negates and a board-wipe.
- **End board:** Ame no Murakumo no Mitsurugi (soft discard-or-negate + re-summon wipe) · Futsu no Mitama no Mitsurugi · set Mitsurugi Great Purification / Tempest / Prayers; often a Rank-8 (Titanic Galaxy).
- **Choke:** **Droll & Lock Bird** is the single best card — they make multiple searches per turn; Droll after the first add nearly bricks them. Ash the first searcher (Aramasa/Kusanagi/Saji). Belle hits GY revival.
- **First:** Don't fold to one negate/wipe. Keep a **spare card** to feed Murakumo's discard so your key effect resolves. End on disruption that doesn't need monsters on board (Murakumo's wipe whiffs).
- **Second:** **Nibiru** (discard it to dodge Murakumo's gate; tribute their board after self-revives). Non-targeting/non-destruction wipes (Kaiju/Lava Golem) get under trap negates. Bait Great Purification first.
- **Handtraps/side:** Droll (best), Ash, Nibiru, Ghost Belle; side Dimensional Barrier / stun (Rivalry/Gozen — mono-Reptile).
- **Confidence: high** (~4.6% of meta, multiple Top 8s).

## Mitsurugi-Ryzeal — *tier 2 ritual + Rank-4 Xyz*
- **How it wins:** Mitsurugi grind core + a Rank-4 Ryzeal package (**Ryzeal Detonator** = free repeatable destroy). More going-second power, more layers.
- **End board:** Ame no Murakumo (+ Prayers set) · 4-material Ryzeal Detonator · D/D/D Wave High King Caesar · Mitsurugi traps; Habakiri in hand.
- **Choke:** **Ash / Imperm on ICE Ryzeal** — pilots openly fear "Ash or Imperm on Ice" (stops the line with no follow-up). Droll/Ash the Mitsurugi search too.
- **First:** As Mitsurugi; respect Detonator's free destroy on your turn — bait its detach with a low-value card first.
- **Second:** Nibiru. Detonator is a *destroy* effect → use non-destruction (Kaiju/Lava Golem/bounce) so you don't feed it. Clear Murakumo without triggering its wipe on yourself.
- **Handtraps/side:** Imperm (on Ice), Ash, Droll, Nibiru.
- **Confidence: medium** (smaller slice than pure Mitsurugi; Top 8 Asia WCQ / Mexico).

## Maliss — *tier 1 Cyberse Link / banish combo*
- **How it wins:** Special Summons monsters back from **banishment** to chain Link bosses + a self-recurring loop, then grinds with the Maliss <C> traps. Can OTK with Accesscode.
- **End board:** Maliss Q Hearts Crypter (Quick banish that **can't be negated** while it points to a monster) · Maliss in the Mirror · set Maliss <C> TB-11 / MTP-07 (live the turn they're set).
- **Choke:** **Maliss P White Rabbit** (one-card starter → sets a Maliss Trap). Spot-negate/Ash it. The engine folds to spot removal and "isn't great at playing through established boards." Banish floodgates are **weak** (they recur from banish).
- **First:** Build **hard negates / spot removal**, not banish hate. Their traps need a face-up Maliss monster — pre-emptively removing it turns the traps off. Dimension Shifter is strong.
- **Second:** Clear set <C> traps first (remove their on-board Maliss to brick them). **Hearts Crypter's banish can't be negated** when it points to a monster — disrupt it on activation/cost or remove it before committing. DRNM/Droplet push.
- **Handtraps/side:** Maxx "C" (best), Ash, Imperm, Dimension Shifter, Nibiru.
- **Confidence: medium-high** (Top 8 Team YCS Las Vegas).

## Yummy (Azamina) — *tier 1 Beast Synchro → Fusion negates*
- **How it wins:** Cheap LIGHT-Beast Synchro swarm (Cupsy/Cooky/Lollipo + Yummy Way Synchros) that, in the Azamina/Fiendsmith build, converts into multiple Fusion omni-negates.
- **End board:** Azamina Ilia Silvia (tribute-to-negate) · Fiendsmith's Desirae (recurring negate) · S:P Little Knight · a Yummy Field Spell (from Snatchy).
- **Choke:** **Maxx "C" / Imperm the first Synchro** is cleanest; Ash the Cupsy Way search or the Fiendsmith GY setup. **Yummy Snatchy's Field-Spell placement can't be Ash'd.**
- **First:** Combo deck → Maxx C / Mulcharmy pressure is strong. The Azamina layer dodges off-Standby handtraps, so end on timing-independent disruption. Dimension Shifter wrecks the Fiendsmith GY engine.
- **Second:** **No-negate sweepers** shine — Dark Ruler No More turns off the Fusion negates; Forbidden Droplet neuters Snatchy + omni-negates. **Bystials** banish their LIGHT Fiendsmith GY. Remove Ilia Silvia / Desirae before your main play.
- **Handtraps/side:** Maxx "C", Ash, Dimension Shifter, Bystial Druiswurm, DRNM.
- **Confidence: medium-high** (Top 16 Mexico Nationals).

## Fairy Tail Magistus — *tier 1 spellcaster equip control*
- **How it wins:** Magistus equip package + Fairy Tail control. Fairy Tail - Wiccat ("double Foolish") fuels a search/recursion loop; ends on negates, grinds with Snow + equips.
- **End board:** Teller of the Magistus (omni-negate) · Zoroa, the Magistus Verethragna (negate a monster effect, then destroy a card) · Fairy Tail - Snow recursion.
- **Choke:** **Ash Fairy Tail - Wiccat's send** (kicks out the main engine). Imperm/Veiler the first Magistus/Fairy Tail effect. **Droll is NOT great here** (low-ceiling plays + Called by / Verre Magic).
- **First:** They out-negate your handtraps (Teller/Zoroa). Lead with a board they can't simply out-negate; pressure with Maxx C. Dimension Shifter hits their GY recursion.
- **Second:** Deal with **Teller (omni-negate) first** — bait or remove before your key play. No-negate breakers (DRNM, Droplet, Evenly into backrow/equips) push through; clear Magistus equips first.
- **Handtraps/side:** Ash (Wiccat), Imperm, Nibiru, Dimension Shifter, DRNM.
- **Confidence: medium-high** (Omaha Regional 1st; the YCS Columbus list you shared).

## Sky Striker — *tier 1 spell-based control*
- **How it wins:** Loops **Sky Striker Mobilize - Engage!** via the Ace monsters to out-resource you, disrupts with Widow Anchor / Shark Cannon. Wants its **own monster zones empty**.
- **End board:** Sky Striker Ace - Shizuku + 4–5 set cards (Widow Anchor / Shark Cannon / Eagle Booster), Spells in GY. Low/empty monster board on purpose.
- **Choke:** **Droll & Lock Bird** (best) — chokes the Engage snowball; Ash negates an Engage; Cursed Seal permanently locks it.
- **First:** A clogged board punishes **them** — their Quick-Plays need them to control no monsters, so forcing a monster onto **their** field (Kaiju/Lava Golem/token) shuts off Widow Anchor + Shark Cannon on your turn. Respect Widow Anchor on your key monster.
- **Second:** Little to physically "break" — fight over resources + backrow. Clear set Mecha Spells; put a monster on their field (Kaiju); Ghost Reaper can pre-banish Kagari.
- **Handtraps/side:** Droll (best), Ash, Lava Golem/Kaiju, Ghost Reaper, Lightning Storm.
- **Confidence: high** (well-established; reprinted via Legendary Modern Decks 2026).

## White Forest (Azamina) — *tier 1 synchro spellcaster control*
- **How it wins:** Synchro Spellcasters/Illusions whose Special Summons + Sinful Spoils cards trigger White Forest spells to grind, then lock you out with a stacked board. Azamina splash adds a recyclable negate.
- **End board:** Azamina Ilia Silvia (omni-negate) · Chaos Angel (non-targeting banish) · S:P Little Knight · set Sinful Spoils of the White Forest. (Rciela gives their Synchros protection.)
- **Choke:** Imperm/Ash the early starter (**Elzette / Silvy**); **Droll** chokes the search loop; Ash the Ilia Silvia / Hallowed Azamina line. They turn **dead handtraps into fuel**, so one rarely ends them.
- **First:** Bait Ilia Silvia's omni-negate (it costs a tribute) on a non-essential effect, then resolve your payoff. Expect a non-targeting banish, so don't rely on one fragile piece.
- **Second:** Clear the omni-negate first. Use **non-targeting / non-destruction** removal (Rciela/Illusion protection): Kaiju, Lava Golem, send/banish, Evenly. Avoid pure destruction. Watch Silvera's Book of Eclipse.
- **Handtraps/side:** Droll, Ash, **Effect Veiler** (cleaner than Imperm — they fuel off Imperm), Evenly, Droplet.
- **Confidence: high** (Top 8 UK Open Liverpool; core meta).

## Elfnote — *tier 1–2 synchro fairies (Power Patron)*
- **How it wins:** "Singer" fairies shuffle into the center zone to trigger + Synchro-climb. **Elfnote Power Patron** raises a center monster's Level +3 and immediately Synchros — laddering into Synchro negates.
- **End board:** Junora the Power Patron of Tuning (**negates effects of ALL the opponent's face-up cards**) · Elfnote Seraphim Strelitzia · Baronne de Fleur · set Rhapsodia of Madness.
- **Choke:** **Elfnote Power Patron** (the Level-up + Synchro + search). Imperm/Veiler/Ash it before Junora's wide negate is online. **Maxx C / Droll / Fuwalos wreck it** (over-extends + searches).
- **First:** Junora negates your whole **face-up** field, not one card — commit interruption as **set/face-down** disruption, not a face-up negate. Force Power Patron out early, then handtrap it.
- **Second:** Junora only negates **effects** of face-up cards — stats remain, so **attack over it** or use removal that doesn't activate into the negate (Lava Golem/Kaiju tribute). Clear Junora/Strelitzia. Droplet is strong.
- **Handtraps/side:** Maxx "C", Droll, Nibiru, Effect Veiler, Forbidden Droplet.
- **Confidence: high** on engine / medium on the exact stock board (varies by build).

## Power Patron / Artmage — *tier 2 fusion ladder into negates*
> Almost always the **Power Patron Artmage hybrid** — pure Artmage as its own top deck is largely unverified; side the same package for both.
- **How it wins:** Medius the Pure adds/Special Summons Artmage Power Patron, Fusion-summons up the line, closes on **Nerva** (destruction-immune *while a Field Spell is active*) + the Nervedo Pendulum negate.
- **End board:** Nerva the Power Patron of Creation · Medius the Pure · Artmage Power Patron · (Artmage negate trap) · a Field Spell keeping Nerva immune.
- **Choke:** **Ash / Droll on Medius the Pure** (the search that assembles the Fusion). Imperm/Veiler on Medius or the first Power Patron. Disrupting the **Field Spell** strips Nerva's immunity.
- **First:** Their negate (Nervedo) triggers off **their** plays → on your turn you mostly fear held handtraps. Set up normally; don't walk a key effect into a held negate.
- **Second:** Nerva is destruction-immune only while a Field Spell is active — **clear the Field Spell** (Cosmic Cyclone / Feather Duster) or use **non-destruction** (banish/bounce/Kaiju/Lava Golem) to ignore the immunity. (Artmage: beware the trap that bounces all your S/T if they control 3+ monster Types.)
- **Handtraps/side:** Ash, Droll, Nibiru, Cosmic Cyclone, Lava Golem.
- **Confidence: medium** (Top 8/16 YCS Columbus as the hybrid).

## Radiant Typhoon — *tier 1 Quick-Play-Spell control*
- **How it wins:** Its monsters trigger off **activating quick-play Spells** (Super Poly, Forbidden Droplet, Called by). Grinds with searchers + a monster-negate boss, recycles quick-plays, wins the long game. Often a **Runick** hybrid.
- **End board:** Varuroon (monster-effect negate) · Radiant Typhoon Mandate (turns MST into an omni-negate) · Totem Bird · Shiina, Twin Tempests · 2–4 set Spells.
- **Choke:** Imperm/Ash **Radiant Typhoon Krosea** (double-searcher) and **Meghala** (the only deck-Special-Summon). Deny those and the setup chokes.
- **First:** They hold quick-play breakers (Super Poly, Droplet/Crown) — **don't over-commit into Super Polymerization**; diversify your monster types/attributes to dodge it. Play around Varuroon's negate + Mandate.
- **Second:** Watch **Shiina** (bounces all face-up monsters except itself; or all S/T). Bait Varuroon's monster-negate first, then handle backrow. **Denko Sekka / Anti-Spell Fragrance / Anti-Magic Arrows** shut down their Spell-heavy plan.
- **Handtraps/side:** Ash, Imperm (Krosea/Meghala), Denko Sekka, Anti-Spell Fragrance, Droll.
- **Confidence: high** (1st YCS Sydney; Top 64 France) — *not* obscure.

## Vanquish Soul (K9) — *tier 2 hand-resource beatdown*
- **How it wins:** Reveal monsters in hand to fuel attribute effects, tag fighters in/out, grind resources. **K9** adds bodies + punishes handtraps.
- **End board:** Vanquish Soul Caesar Valius (Quick destroy; can make itself unaffected by revealing EARTH) · a Rank-4 (Pluton HG line) · **K9-17 Ripper** (negates monster effects from hand/GY).
- **Choke:** Ash **Vanquish Soul Razen** (main searcher) — but **K9-17 Ripper negates your Ash/Veiler from hand**, so lead with **Infinite Impermanence** (a Trap → dodges Ripper) or hold the monster handtrap until Ripper's spent. Their tag-out dodges targeted disruption.
- **First:** Grind/beatdown deck, not an OTK — a normal disruptive board is fine. Respect Caesar's free destroy + EARTH "unaffected" mode. Force them to commit before you tag interruption.
- **Second:** Attrition + non-targeting removal. **Forbidden Droplet / DRNM** turn off Caesar/K9 negates; **Evenly** is great (they go wide). Caesar can become unaffected → prefer mass non-targeting over single-target.
- **Handtraps/side:** Imperm (slips past Ripper), Droll, Thunder King Rai-Oh (anti-reveal), Forbidden Droplet, Evenly; stun (Rivalry/Gozen).
- **Confidence: medium** (~2.75%; Top 8 Team YCS Las Vegas as the K9 hybrid).

## Lunalight — *rogue fusion combo*
- **How it wins:** A one-card Gold Leo line builds a sticky **Liger Dancer** board going first, then double-attacks + grinds via GY recursion.
- **End board:** 2× Lunalight Liger Dancer · Lunalight Silver Hound (banish self + a Fusion to negate a S/T) · S:P Little Knight or I:P Masquerena.
- **Choke:** **Imperm on Lunalight Gold Leo** (one-card starter) + **Ash on Lunalight Silver Hound**. Disrupt **before Liger resolves** — once Liger lands you usually lose. Droll alone isn't enough.
- **First:** Hold interaction for the Liger pieces. Build a board that survives a Liger wipe (it only destroys **Special-Summoned** monsters — a Normal-Summoned body or continuous floodgate survives).
- **Second:** Liger is **unaffected by non-Lunalight effects** — targeted removal/negation bounces off. Out it with **tribute-based** removal: **Kaiju / Lava Golem / Ra Sphere Mode**. Don't flood Special-Summoned monsters into an unanswered Liger.
- **Handtraps/side:** Imperm (Gold Leo), Ash (Silver Hound), Lava Golem/Kaiju, Nibiru, Dimension Shifter.
- **Confidence: medium-low** (rogue / locals-strong; Top 8 Victoria States).

## Predaplant — *rogue fusion toolbox*
- **How it wins:** Albaz/Branded fusion toolbox — spreads **Predator Counters** (turning your monsters into Lv1 fusion fodder), negates with Dragostapelia, OTKs with Triphyovertum. Mostly Genesys/OCG in TCG.
- **End board:** Predaplant Dragostapelia (negates an opp monster with a Predator Counter) · Mirrorjade · Branded Fusion / Super Polymerization held open.
- **Choke:** Ash **Branded Fusion** / first big Fusion; Imperm the counter-making Predaplant. **Super Polymerization is NOT Ash-able** — play around it.
- **First:** Their **Super Poly** can eat your monsters on your turn — avoid presenting two same-attribute monsters that complete a fusion. Their pure board is weak; a normal board holds.
- **Second:** Remove Dragostapelia (non-targeting/banish) or attack over it once its negate is spent; Mirrorjade caveat applies (Super Poly/banish). Your own Super Poly is great vs their fat bodies.
- **Handtraps/side:** Ash, Imperm, Nibiru, Dimension Shifter, Super Polymerization.
- **Confidence: low** for standard TCG (rogue/Genesys) — specifics extrapolated from shared Albaz weaknesses.

## Clown Crew — *rogue Tribute-Summon floodgate control*
> **Correction:** "Concours de Cuisine (Culinary Confrontation)" in the list is a **Nouvelles/Patissciel** ritual splash, not a Clown Crew card. The real Clown Ritual Spell is **Clown Crew Rehearsal**.
- **How it wins:** Makes disposable tribute fodder, Tribute Summons **Clown Crew Biancaviso** (draw / negate face-up cards) **and Tribute-Summon-only floodgates (Vanity's Ruler / Vanity's Fiend)** to lock you out, then grinds. Often prefers to go second.
- **End board:** Vanity's Fiend (neither player can Special Summon) or Vanity's Ruler (you can't) · Clown Crew Biancaviso · set Clown traps; sometimes Super Starslayer TY-PHON.
- **Choke:** Ash the **Clown Crew Rehearsal / Biancaviso** line (fodder + floodgate engine); Ghost Belle the GY-banish Ritual effect. Droll hurts. **No fodder = no Vanity tribute.**
- **First:** Resolve your board **before** they Tribute a Vanity floodgate, and end on disruption that doesn't need to Special Summon (set traps / in-hand handtraps).
- **Second — the key:** the lock is the Vanity **Special-Summon floodgate**. **Don't fight it with Special Summons.** Use **non-SS outs**: Dark Ruler No More / Forbidden Droplet (turn off the body), **Evenly Matched** (ignores it), or Imperm to open a SS window. Remove the floodgate, *then* commit Special Summons.
- **Handtraps/side:** Ash, Ghost Belle, **DRNM / Forbidden Droplet / Evenly Matched** (non-SS outs to the floodgate).
- **Confidence: low-medium** (rogue; not in headline tier list, shows up as Clown Crew hybrids).

## How to use this in the app
1. **Settings → ⚡ Load meta decks** — imports all 17 opponents + the pre-filled "Meta - May 2026" format. (Re-run anytime to refresh to the latest research.)
2. **Format tab** — pick **your** deck as primary, open each matchup; the chokepoint / game-plan / end board / handtraps are already filled. Add your own notes after games.
3. **Testing → Going second** — choose an opponent, click **⚡ Use their typical board**, and drill breaking it (the end boards above are pre-seeded).
4. **Tournament journal** (Format → a tournament) — log rounds; per-matchup win-rates aggregate so you learn which matchups to practise.
